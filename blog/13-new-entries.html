🚨 13 New Submissions 🚨 And a Full Glow Up

Written by Compliance Liaison · May 10, 2025

It’s been a big week at Submit Relentlessly HQ. We’ve pushed a major update to the site that includes new proposals, better navigation, clearer language, and a freshly tuned homepage that answers the question: what even are regulations, and why should I care?

Spoiler: You should care. A lot.

🆕 13 New Deregulation Suggestions Just Dropped

We’ve added a brand new batch of proposals across three categories — covering everything from FDA reform to Congressional accountability to, yes, the understaffing of the FAA (you know, the folks keeping the planes from colliding).

We're up to 61 reform submissions as of right now, and we're not slowing down.

  • Let FDA Use the Fees It Collects
  • End Staffing Limits That Cripple the FDA
  • Make Companies Accountable for Device Safety

  • Restore Digital Free Speech Protections

🗳️ Democratic Reform

🧮 A Sortable Index Has Arrived

You asked, we heard you: the Sortable Submission Index is here.

It includes every proposal on the site — sortable by topic, spiciness, plausibility, and more. We even included our personal ✨cursed administrative prophecies✨ about what’s likely, what’s radical, and what might get you quietly removed from a legislative working group.

If you disagree with our rankings, that’s valid. Let us know at /contact.

✏️ Cleaner Titles, Fewer Weeds

Policy Pam had a little too much fun with legal phrasing in the first draft of the site. We’ve gone through all the submission titles and made them clearer, punchier, and more human-friendly. Same ideas — just less jargon.

🏠 Homepage Refresh

We’ve fully updated the homepage to include a quick primer:

  • What are regulations?
  • How do they relate to laws?
  • Why should I care about this portal?
  • What is this delightful chaos I’ve stumbled into?

(P.S. We’re still debugging a weird spacing issue. It’s fine. Everything’s fine.)

🔧 Other Tweaks and Touch-Ups

We made a bunch of other minor site-wide upgrades to improve layout, accessibility, and general usability. You'll feel it more than you'll see it — but the site should just work better now.
