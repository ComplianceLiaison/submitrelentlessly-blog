New Submissions Just Dropped—And They Cut Deep

Written by Compliance Liaison · May 21, 2025

🧹 Fire the Officials Who Betray the Constitution

Sometimes it’s not enough to vote them out. Some appointees obstruct lawful transitions of power.

Some ignore court orders. Some sabotage investigations or suppress urgent warnings.

And yet—they stay in office. Why? Because the Senior Executive Service (SES) protections that were

designed to insulate federal leadership from political whims now serve as a shield for egregious misconduct.

📝 Submit: Enable Removal of High-Ranking Officials for Constitutional Violations

aka—Fire the Deep State Traitors

This reform adds a narrow exception for expedited removal of SES officials when credible findings show they

violated constitutional duties. It protects whistleblowers and civil servants—and ensures bad actors don’t get a golden parachute.

💸 Close the Capital Gains Loopholes and Fund Public Care

Every time you hear “we can’t afford Medicare expansion” or “Medicaid is unsustainable,” remember this:

we spend far more on tax breaks for billionaires than we do on public health.

📝 Submit: Close Capital Gains Loopholes to Fund Medicare & Medicaid

aka—End Bureaucratic Waste in Tax Expenditures

This proposal eliminates tax timing tricks and carve-outs that let the wealthy defer or avoid paying their fair share.

It doesn’t just raise revenue—it restores fairness, and creates the fiscal space to strengthen the safety net we’re told we can’t afford.

🚨 Make FEMA Aid Fair Again

Disaster relief is supposed to be fast, equitable, and needs-based. In reality? FEMA’s discretionary process

favors wealthy zip codes, penalizes renters, and leaves already-marginalized communities in ruin.

📝 Submit: Restore Equitable Access to Federal Disaster Relief Funds

aka—Eliminate FEMA’s Discretionary Red Tape

This reform cuts the administrative “flexibility” that lets disaster aid mirror redlining maps.

It replaces opaque criteria with clear, equitable distribution rules—and demands accountability for how recovery funds are awarded.
